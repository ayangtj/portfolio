Brave Wallet Onboarding Evaluation

Research Study on Improving Crypto Wallet Onboarding

Overview

Cryptocurrency wallets can be intimidating for new users. Brave Browser wanted to improve their wallet onboarding experience to make crypto more accessible to mainstream users while maintaining security best practices.

The Challenge

Crypto wallet onboarding faces a unique challenge: balancing ease-of-use with security education. Users need to understand concepts like private keys and seed phrases without becoming overwhelmed or taking risky shortcuts.

Research Approach

Our comprehensive evaluation included:

  • Usability testing with 12 participants across different crypto experience levels
  • Comparative analysis of 5 leading wallet onboarding flows
  • Heuristic evaluation against crypto-specific usability principles
  • Analysis of drop-off points in the existing funnel

Key Findings

Our research uncovered several critical insights:

  • 63% of crypto beginners were confused by technical terminology during onboarding
  • Users often skipped critical security steps when they felt the process was too lengthy
  • The seed phrase backup step had the highest drop-off rate (41%)
  • Contextual help was highly valued but often difficult to find
  • Users wanted verification that they had completed security steps correctly

Recommendations & Impact

Based on our findings, we recommended several key improvements:

  • Progressive education: Introduce concepts just before they're needed
  • Visual metaphors to explain abstract security concepts
  • Interactive verification of seed phrase backup
  • Simplified technical language with contextual glossary
  • Clearer indication of progress and estimated time to complete

Implementation results:

  • 28% reduction in onboarding abandonment rate
  • 33% increase in successful seed phrase verifications
  • Positive sentiment in user feedback increased by 45%
