Healthcare AI Predictive Analytics Platform

Jobs To Be Done - UX Research Case Study

Overview

The healthcare industry relies heavily on data-driven decisions, yet many healthcare providers struggle with disconnected data systems and inefficient processes. This case study examines how we developed a comprehensive AI-powered data platform to help healthcare organizations better understand patient needs, optimize operations, and improve outcomes.

The Challenge

Healthcare organizations are drowning in data but starving for insights. Our client needed a platform that could unify disparate data sources, generate actionable predictions, and facilitate better decision-making across their organization.

Research Approach

We conducted extensive user research to understand the jobs to be done for different healthcare stakeholders. This included:

  • 15+ in-depth interviews with healthcare administrators, physicians, and analysts
  • Competitive analysis of existing healthcare analytics solutions
  • Contextual inquiry sessions observing current workflow processes
  • Jobs-to-be-done framework analysis to identify key user needs

Key Findings

Our research revealed several critical insights:

  • Healthcare professionals spent 40% of their time searching for and compiling data
  • Existing systems provided backward-looking analysis but lacked predictive capabilities
  • Decision-makers struggled to translate complex data into concrete action plans
  • Teams needed collaborative tools to align on data interpretations

Solution & Impact

Based on our research, we designed and developed a unified healthcare analytics platform that:

  • Consolidated multiple data sources into a single, intuitive dashboard
  • Implemented predictive AI models to forecast patient outcomes and resource needs
  • Created role-specific interfaces for different stakeholders
  • Developed collaborative tools for team-based decision making

The platform resulted in:

  • 32% reduction in time spent gathering and analyzing data
  • 18% improvement in resource allocation efficiency
  • Positive feedback from 87% of users on the platform's usability
