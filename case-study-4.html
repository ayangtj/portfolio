Jobs To Be Done Framework - Healthcare AI Platform

Applying JTBD to Optimize Healthcare Delivery with AI

Background

In 2024, I worked with a leading healthcare technology company to improve their AI-powered clinical decision support platform. This artificial intelligence (AI) platform enables healthcare networks and clinicians to optimize care delivery through predictive analytics. It is designed to help hospital networks reduce costs while maintaining high-quality care standards.

Challenges

Despite significant technological capabilities, the platform faced low adoption rates among clinicians. The primary challenges included:

  • Workflow integration issues - The AI tools weren't seamlessly fitting into existing clinical workflows
  • Trust barriers - Clinicians were hesitant to rely on AI-generated insights for patient care decisions
  • Value perception gap - The platform's benefits weren't clearly understood in relation to clinical outcomes

Research Objectives

  1. Understand the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of clinicians' jobs
  2. Identify key moments where AI could provide meaningful support in the clinical workflow
  3. Discover unmet needs that would drive adoption of AI-assisted decision making

Research Methods

  • Contextual Inquiry - Observing clinicians in their work environment
  • JTBD Interviews - Focused on understanding the jobs clinicians are trying to accomplish
  • Journey Mapping - Documenting the end-to-end clinical decision process

Jobs To Be Done Framework Implementation

We applied the Jobs To Be Done (JTBD) framework to uncover the underlying motivations and goals of clinicians, beyond just their functional tasks. This approach helped us understand:

  • What "jobs" clinicians were "hiring" various tools to accomplish
  • The circumstances that triggered the need for assistance
  • The desired outcomes from both functional and emotional perspectives

Participant Profile

Primary Care Physicians

  • Manage diverse patient conditions
  • High patient volume
  • Limited time per patient

Specialists

  • Deep expertise in specific areas
  • Complex decision-making
  • Research-oriented approach

Hospital Administrators

  • Focus on operational efficiency
  • Resource allocation concerns
  • Quality metrics oversight

Key Jobs Uncovered

1. Make confident decisions with incomplete information

Job: Reduce decision-making uncertainty

Clinicians regularly face situations where they must make decisions without all the ideal information available.

Pain point: Anxiety about missing critical factors that could affect patient outcomes

Quote: "I'm often making decisions based on partial information and limited time. What keeps me up at night is worrying I've missed something important."

2. Stay current with evolving medical knowledge

Job: Maintain expertise without overwhelming time investment

Clinicians struggle to keep up with the rapid pace of medical research while managing full patient loads.

Pain point: Fear of falling behind on latest treatments and protocols

Quote: "It's impossible to read everything in my field. I need ways to quickly understand what new research actually matters for my patients."

3. Demonstrate evidence-based reasoning for decisions

Job: Build trust with patients and colleagues

Clinicians need to justify their decisions with clear rationales to various stakeholders.

Pain point: Difficulty articulating complex medical reasoning quickly

Quote: "Patients want to know why I'm recommending something, and I need to explain complex decisions in terms they can understand and trust."

Opportunity Areas

Based on our JTBD analysis, we identified key opportunity areas for the AI platform:

High Priority

  • Clinical pattern recognition - Highlighting similar cases and outcomes
  • Contextual information summarization - Surfacing relevant patient history at decision points
  • Evidence-based recommendation support - Providing research backing for treatment options

Medium Priority

  • Documentation assistance - Reducing administrative burden
  • Patient-friendly explanation generation - Helping translate medical concepts
  • Treatment plan visualization - Making complex plans more understandable

Future Exploration

  • Predictive risk modeling - Anticipating potential complications
  • Cross-specialty knowledge integration - Breaking down specialty silos
  • Personalized continuing education - Targeting learning to practice patterns

Platform Redesign Principles

Our JTBD research informed several key design principles for the platform redesign:

  1. Workflow integration first - AI features must fit naturally within existing clinical processes
  2. Transparent reasoning - Always show the evidence and logic behind AI recommendations
  3. Progressive disclosure - Present information in layers of increasing detail as needed
  4. Human-AI collaboration - Position AI as enhancing rather than replacing clinical judgment
  5. Contextual relevance - Ensure insights are specific to the patient's unique situation

Impact

Metric Before After Change
Platform adoption rate 23% 62% +39%
Daily active users 156 412 +164%
Feature utilization 1.4 features/session 3.8 features/session +171%
User satisfaction score 2.8/5 4.3/5 +54%

Learnings & Reflection

The Jobs To Be Done framework provided crucial insights that a traditional feature-focused approach would have missed. By understanding the deeper motivations and contexts of clinical decision-making, we were able to reposition the AI platform as a trusted assistant rather than a mysterious algorithm.

Key learnings from this project include:

  • Healthcare AI adoption requires addressing emotional needs (confidence, trust) as much as functional ones
  • Clinicians are willing to embrace AI that respects their expertise while reducing cognitive load
  • Explaining AI reasoning in clinical terms rather than technical ones significantly increases trust
  • Different clinical specialties have unique JTBD profiles that require tailored approaches

Next Steps

Building on the success of this initial redesign, the team is now:

  1. Expanding the JTBD research to additional clinical specialties
  2. Developing specialty-specific AI modules based on unique workflow needs
  3. Creating an integrated feedback loop that continuously improves AI recommendations based on clinician input
  4. Exploring ways to extend the platform's value by connecting clinician jobs with patient jobs
